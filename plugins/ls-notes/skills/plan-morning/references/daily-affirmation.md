# Writing a Daily Affirmation

## What It Is

A daily affirmation is a simple statement that **defines you as the person you want to be**. Writing it each morning primes your brain to start building that belief — and with repetition, the change forms from within. (The Five Minute Journal cites a Harvard study where hotel staff primed to see their work as exercise actually lost weight, without doing anything differently.)

## How to Write One

- Begin with **"I am…"** and phrase it in the **present tense**, as if it's already true.
- Make it about **who you want to become**, not a task or a thing you want to get.
- Keep it **believable but stretchy** — just past your comfort zone, not so far you can't buy it.
- Keep it **positive and personal** — what you are, not what you're avoiding.
- **Litmus test:** notice how you feel after writing it. A good one leaves you a little more confident.

## Examples

- I am someone who takes care of my body and mind.
- I am focused, and I finish what I start.
- I am calm under pressure and I handle hard things well.
